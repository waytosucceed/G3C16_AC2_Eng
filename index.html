Conjunctions


Convention/Grammar

Conjunction: A word that connects words, phrases, or clauses in a sentence.

Coordinating conjunction: A conjunction (e.g., and, but, or) that joins two independent clauses or elements of equal importance.

Subordinating conjunction: A conjunction (e.g., because, since, although) that introduces a dependent clause and connects it to an independent clause.

Independent clause: A clause that can stand alone as a complete sentence because it contains a subject and a predicate. It expresses a complete thought.

Dependent clause: A clause that cannot stand alone as a complete sentence because it lacks either a subject or a predicate or both. It depends on an independent clause to form a complete sentence.



Words that I learnt from the story

Intervention: The act of interfering in a situation, often to prevent or modify a result.
Sentence: The teacher's intervention helped resolve the conflict between the students.

Testament: A statement or action that serves as proof or evidence of something.
Sentence: Her dedication to her studies is a testament to her hard work and determination.

Evidence: Facts or information indicating whether a belief or proposition is valid.
Sentence: The police found evidence at the crime scene.
